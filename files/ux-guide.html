WIMC Design System & Screen Specifications

Design decisions, component patterns, interaction notes, and developer handoff documentation for the full WIMC UX prototype.

8 screens delivered
2 themes (dark + editorial)
3 user types

Product Vision

WIMC is a creator platform for urban experiences — walks, workshops, food tours, pop-ups. It sits at the intersection of Linktree (creator pages), Eventbrite (ticketing), and Airbnb Experiences (discovery), but rooted in Indian cities and their local creator culture.

The product serves three audiences simultaneously: Creators who host experiences, Explorers (the public) who discover and book them, and Venue owners (Adda) who provide the spaces.

Core Design Principle
Creators need to feel professional. Explorers need to feel excited. The design system reflects this: a focused dark professional environment for creators, and a warm editorial aesthetic for the public-facing side.

User Personas

The Creator

Urban photographers, food bloggers, architects, musicians — people who already run experiences informally and want to professionalise. They care about looking credible to their audience and not losing money to no-shows. The Creator Studio must be efficient and make them feel in control.

The Explorer

Young professionals (22–38) in Tier 1/2 Indian cities. They want to do something different on weekends — not a restaurant or a mall. They discover through Instagram and WhatsApp. The public pages must feel editorial and warm, not corporate.

The Adda Owner

Café, studio, or rooftop operators with underutilised hours. They want incremental revenue without the complexity of managing event logistics. The Adda dashboard must make proposal review and calendar management frictionless.

Two Themes

Theme 1: Creator Studio (Dark)

Used on all screens the creator sees: Dashboard, My Page, Events, Tier, Venues, Adda Dashboard. Base colour #0A0A0B with raised surface layers. Colour pops come from the 5-colour brand palette applied contextually.

Theme 2: Editorial Light (Explorer)

Used on public-facing screens (Discovery feed, Event Page). Base is #F7F2E8 (chalk) with ink text #1A1108. Feels like a well-designed city magazine. Colour pops from turmeric, gulaal, neel — Indian ink-pigment palette.

Theme Transition
Creators switch between the two worlds when they preview their public page. The "View live page" link in the My Page editor should open in a separate browser tab. The transition from dark studio → light editorial should feel like stepping out of the office.

Dim / ambient variants

Every brand colour has a dim variant (rgba with ~12–15% alpha) used for backgrounds of pills, icon containers, and highlight areas. Never apply full-opacity brand colours to large background areas — use the dim variant instead to keep the dark base legible.

Surface stack (dark theme)

  • --bg-base #0A0A0B — page background
  • --bg-raised #131318 — sidebar, cards in base context
  • --bg-elevated #1C1C24 — section cards, modals
  • --bg-overlay #242430 — hover states, inner panels
  • --bg-hover #2E2E3C — active hover surfaces

Syne is used exclusively for headlines, card titles, stat numbers, and brand elements. It has a distinctive geometric quality that reads as both modern and rooted. DM Sans is neutral and readable at small sizes. JetBrains Mono for all metadata — dates, prices, counts, handles — gives the product a technical-but-warm character.

Font loading
Load all three from Google Fonts in a single <link> tag. Include display=swap to prevent invisible text during load. All three are available as variable fonts — load them as variable for best performance.

Spacing & Radius

  • Base unit: 4px. All spacing is multiples of 4.
  • Section padding: 32px (desktop), 20px (mobile)
  • Card internal padding: 20–28px depending on density
  • Gap between cards/sections: 20–24px
  • Sidebar width: 220px (fixed)
  • Topbar height: 64px (sticky)

Radius scale: --r-sm: 6px (buttons, inputs, small chips), --r-md: 12px (tags, inner panels), --r-lg: 18px (section cards), --r-xl: 24px (modals, hero cards), --r-full: 9999px (pills, status badges, avatar).

Key Components

Navigation sidebar

Fixed 220px sidebar with brand area, grouped nav items, and user footer. Nav groups use uppercase monospace section labels. Active state: dim background + brand-light text. The sidebar accent colour changes per context: coral for Creator Studio, amber for Adda.

Status pills

Used throughout for event status, tier state, and proposal state. Always: dim background + matching border at 20% + matching text. Examples: Live (teal), Almost Full (amber), Draft (overlay/muted), Confirmed (success), Pending (amber).

Progress bars

6–8px height, bg-overlay track, gradient fill matching the relevant brand colour. Used in: tier metrics (coral/teal/amber), stat cards (success), capacity bars in events (teal → amber as fill increases). Always show a label with current/target below.

Modals

Backdrop: rgba(0,0,0,0.7) with backdrop-filter:blur(4px). Modal card: --bg-elevated with 1px default border. Enters with translateY(20px) → 0 transition. Max-width 600px. Always closeable by clicking the backdrop.

Cards (creator side)

Background --bg-elevated, 1px border-default, --r-lg radius. Hover: border-strong + translateY(-2px). Section header: 20px pad, bottom border-subtle, flex space-between. Inner content padding: 20–24px.

01 · Dashboard Overview

The first screen a creator sees after login. It must answer three questions immediately: How are my events doing? Where am I on tier? What needs my attention?

  • Welcome strip — personalised greeting + live page visit count (most emotionally resonant metric). The coral radial glow behind the number should pulse subtly on page load.
  • Stat grid — 4 cards: Events, Attendees, Revenue (MTD), Leads. Each has an icon, large number, and delta. Colour-coded by purpose (coral → teal → amber → neel).
  • Upcoming events — compact rows with date badge, capacity bar, status pill. Clicking opens Events screen.
  • Tier progress — sidebar card showing 4 metrics as micro-progress bars. "Details →" goes to Tier screen.

02 · My Page — Block Editor

The creator's link-in-bio builder. Most important screen for creator identity.

  • Split layout: Left = block management panel, Right = live phone preview. The phone preview must update in real-time as blocks are toggled/edited.
  • Block types: Profile Identity (required, always first), Links & Social, Events (auto-pulled from Events screen), Testimonials, Custom CTA.
  • Toggle behaviour: Toggling a block off hides it from the public page but keeps it in the editor. Visual distinction: off-blocks should be 40% opacity.
  • Expanded edit state: Clicking Edit on a block reveals an inline form below it. Only one block can be expanded at a time.
  • Autosave: Save on every field blur. Show "Saved" indicator (green check + text) in topbar. Show "Saving…" during save.
  • Publish: "Publish Page" button in topbar should show a confirmation that changes are live, with a shareable link.

03 · Events Management

Full event lifecycle management. The create flow is critical — it must be fast and not feel like filling a government form.

  • Tab bar: Upcoming / Drafts / Past / All. Badge counts on Upcoming and Drafts tabs only.
  • Event card anatomy: 4px left accent bar (colour = status), status pill, event title, 3 meta items, stat footer with capacity bar.
  • Create event modal: 4 steps. Step 1: Basics (title, category, city, date/time, venue, description, ticket type). Step 2: Pricing & capacity. Step 3: Media (cover image). Step 4: Review & publish. Progress shown as numbered dots with connector lines.
  • Draft save: Available at any step. Drafts persist across sessions.

04 · Tier Progress

Gamification layer — the most motivational screen. Must feel like a game achievement tracker, not a boring spreadsheet.

  • Hero banner: Current tier name + emoji, overall % progress bar to next tier, next tier name. Amber/coral gradient glow.
  • Roadmap: Horizontal connected node chain. Completed tiers show ✓ + amber fill. Current tier pulses subtly. Locked tiers show 🔒 + muted styling.
  • 4 metric cards: Each shows large current number, target, progress bar, and helper text. Colours: coral (events), teal (attendees), amber (GMV), success-green (rating).
  • Benefits accordion: Categorised by tier. Unlocked = green check + expandable description. Locked = lock icon + tier badge. Clicking unlocked items expands them inline.

05 · Venue Discovery (Adda)

The venue marketplace. Map-first layout — creators think spatially about where they want to host.

  • Layout: Fixed-width list panel (420px) left, map fills remainder. On mobile, stack vertically with toggle between list and map.
  • Map pins: Amber price bubbles with arrow pointer. Selected pin = coral. Clicking a pin selects that venue in the list and opens the detail panel.
  • Detail panel: Slides in from right over the map. 300px wide. Shows photos, specs, available dates (teal chips), amenities, and a disabled booking CTA with tier lock message.
  • Tier lock: A notice bar inside the list panel explaining booking requires Nukkad tier. CTA links to Tier Progress screen. Do not hide the venues — browsing is always available.

06 · Explorer Discovery Feed

The public homepage. Tone shift: warm, editorial, magazine-like. Should make someone feel "I want to go out this weekend."

  • Nav: Dark ink bar. Logo + nav links + city selector + auth buttons. City selector should open a city picker modal.
  • Hero: Dark ink background, large Syne headline, search bar with search button. Topic chips below. The hero should feel like a newspaper front page, not a SaaS landing page.
  • Feed: Two-column masonry grid. Cards vary in thumb height for visual rhythm. Category colour-coded by pill colour.
  • Right sidebar: City filter (pill-style), Trending Creators (follow buttons), Newsletter widget (dark background).

07 · Public Event Page

The conversion screen — from curious to booked. Must handle the full decision journey.

  • Hero: Dark cinematic banner with event emoji as atmospheric element (large, low opacity). Category badge + title + 3 meta items in overlay.
  • Two-column layout: Left = event details (about, key info grid, itinerary, what to bring, host bio, reviews). Right = sticky booking card.
  • Booking card: Price, spots warning (gulaal when low), date/time/quantity controls, running total, primary CTA, cancellation policy, share/save buttons.
  • Itinerary timeline: Numbered dots connected by a vertical line. Dot colours vary by brand palette to create visual interest.
  • Social proof: Reviews section below the fold. Rating stars in amber, reviewer avatar colour-coded.

08 · Adda Dashboard

The venue owner's operations centre. Prioritises incoming proposals and calendar clarity.

  • Amber accent: Adda sidebar uses amber instead of coral (different role = different identity). Brand icon = "A" on amber.
  • Calendar: Mini calendar with amber (booked) and teal (proposal) day highlights. "Block date" lets owners mark unavailability.
  • Proposals: Accept/Decline actions on pending proposals. Accepted proposals show "Confirmed" badge. Proposals auto-appear on the calendar when confirmed.
  • Revenue chart: Simple 6-bar column chart. Only current month uses amber gradient fill — previous months are muted.

Interaction Notes

Transitions

Base transition: 220ms ease. Slow transitions (modals, panels, page reveals): 400ms cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1). Fast transitions (hover backgrounds, color changes): 120ms ease. Never animate layout shifts — only opacity, transform, and colour.

Loading states

Skeleton screens for: Dashboard stats (placeholder rectangles), Events list, Discovery feed. Skeletons should pulse with a subtle opacity animation. Never show spinners for content that can be skeletonised.

Empty states

Every list view needs an empty state: large centred icon + headline + description + primary CTA. Used in Events (no events yet), Leads, Proposals. The empty state CTA should always link to the relevant creation action.

Toast notifications

Bottom-right position. Duration: 4 seconds. Types: success (green left border), warning (amber), error (gulaal). Slide in from bottom-right. Content: short verb phrase + optional "Undo" link for destructive actions.

Responsive Strategy

The prototype is designed at 1280px desktop width. Below are breakpoint notes for implementation.

  • ≥1280px (desktop): All screens as designed.
  • 1024–1279px: Sidebar collapses to icon-only (48px). Content panels reflow to single column where needed. Stats grid → 2×2.
  • ≤768px (mobile): Sidebar replaces with bottom tab bar (5 items: Home, Page, Events, Leads, Profile). All modals become full-screen drawers from bottom. Map+list becomes tabbed toggle. Phone preview in My Page hides — replaced by "Preview" button that opens full-screen preview.

The Explorer public pages are inherently mobile-first — event browsing and booking happens primarily on phone. Prioritise vertical scroll performance and large tap targets (min 44px) on the public pages.

Remaining Screens to Build

These are not in this prototype but are required for a production-ready product:

  • Bookings / Sessions — calendar view for booked attendee sessions, check-in QR codes
  • Leads CRM — email lead table, segmentation, export
  • Testimonials — review collection, display, moderation
  • Analytics — page views, event conversion funnel, revenue over time
  • Settings — Profile — creator personal info, category, city
  • Settings — Payout — bank account, UPI, payout history
  • Creator Onboarding — 4-step wizard from signup to first published page
  • Explorer Profile — saved events, past bookings, following
  • Notification Centre — all in-app alerts with mark-read / filters
  • Adda Onboarding — venue listing creation (5 steps)
  • New Event Wizard — Steps 2–4 — pricing, media, review/publish
